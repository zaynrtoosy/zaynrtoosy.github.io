About

Biomedical Engineering · CAD & Simulation · Orthopaedics

I'm a final-year Biomedical Engineering MEng student at Imperial. Most of what drives me sits at the intersection of CAD, simulation, and clinical application — particularly orthopaedics, where I think engineering has a genuine opportunity to improve people's lives in ways that aren't yet fully realised.

I've taught myself SOLIDWORKS to a level where I can use it practically — FEA, technical drawings, animated assemblies — mostly because the projects I took on needed it. I'm reasonably confident with the tools, and honest about the fact that there's still a great deal I'm learning. What I try to do is close the loop: I've found that the problems you only notice at the physical prototype stage tend to be the most instructive ones.

My clinical experience — years volunteering in a GP practice and a pharmacy — has made the shortcomings in orthopaedic care feel very concrete to me, especially for younger patients. That's made me more curious about the engineering that could address them, and more aware of how much care goes into getting it right.

I'm looking for a role where I can contribute and keep learning — ideally one that connects engineering precision with direct patient benefit. I'm very open to guidance and feedback from people who know the field better than I do.

Technical Toolkit

SOLIDWORKS — FEA, motion studies, animated assemblies, production-ready technical drawings. Self-taught and applied across research and personal projects. Also experienced with MATLAB and Python for data acquisition, signal processing, and diagnostic software. Hands-on with Arduino and RFID for embedded hardware. Comfortable with FDM 3D printing, LaTeX, and DaVinci Resolve for technical documentation and video.
